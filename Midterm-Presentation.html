Cost Categories

ER Charges, by Race and Gender

Data Prep for Modeling

  • For dx1 and dx2, added another column indicating the frequency of that diagnosis
  • Used this freqency column to classify the diagnosis into quartiles (higher quartile = more freqent diagnosis)
  • Created a binary target indicating whether length of stay was at least 4 days
  • Included variables: los_ind, age, pay_ub92, sex, raceethn, provider, moa, admtype, service, quant_dx1, quant_dx2, yoa, payer

Training and Testing

  • Sampled a random 10,000 observations from the prepped data
  • Used 70/30 split for training and testing

Models

  • Decision Tree: 0.5952
  • Random Forest: 0.5911
  • K-Nearest Neighbors: 0.5581
  • GLM Binomial: 0.5817
  • GBM: 0.6040

Tuning

  • Tree:
  • Tried max depths of 3, 7, and 12
  • Used 10-fold cross validation

  • Chose 7 for max depth

  • Forest:
  • Tried # variables 5, 7, and 10
  • Tried min node size of 50, 100, and 150
  • Used 7-fold cross validation repeated 3 times

  • Chose 7 for # variables and 100 for node size

Tuning

  • Tried interaction depth of 3 and 6
  • Tried min observations in node of 50 and 75
  • Chose interaction depth of 3 and min node size of 50
